• Pasta (200–250g)
  • Fresh figs
  • Blue cheese (50g)
  • Walnuts (~30g)
  • Rucola pesto
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Oil

How to cook

Boil the pasta in salted water according to the package instructions. Meanwhile, prepare the figs: rinse and slice them into quarters or wedges. Crumble the blue cheese (gorgonzola, roquefort, etc.). Lightly toast the walnuts in a dry skillet, then chop them (but not too finely). Drain the pasta when it’s ready, reserving about 2–3 tablespoons of the cooking water. In a large skillet, heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil (or use a little bit of the reserved pasta water instead for fewer calories). Stir in 2–3 tablespoons of the arugula pesto (or to taste) and the reserved pasta water to create a light sauce. Add the cooked pasta to the skillet and toss to coat evenly with the pesto. Season with salt and pepper if needed. Add the sliced figs and chopped walnuts, mixing gently. Top with the crumbled blue cheese (let it slightly melt if you like) or serve cheese on the side.
